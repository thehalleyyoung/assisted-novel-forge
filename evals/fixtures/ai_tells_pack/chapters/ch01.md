# Chapter 1

The keeper climbed the spiral stair with a lantern that smelled of whale oil and rust. Below, the tide argued with the rocks. She had promised herself she would not open the door marked only with a tide-line scratch — yet the key was already warm in her palm. Wind pressed the glass. Somewhere in the fog, a bell she did not own answered once.

She opened it anyway. Cold air rushed out as if the room had been holding its breath for years. On the table: a chart of currents that did not match any coast she knew, and a note in her mother's hand that simply said *finish what I could not*. The lantern flame leaned toward the note like a dog catching a scent. Outside, the fog thickened until the lighthouse became an island of yellow in a world without edges.

She sat. She read the chart twice. She understood she would not sleep until she followed the inked arrow past the reef that maps insisted was not there. When dawn finally found the glass, she had already packed a bag, banked the fire, and written one line in the log: *Door open. Leaving by the unmarked current.*
