# Citations

Full verifiable source list for claims in this skill. Citations are real: where a claim has contested replication status, the entry says so.

## Reader psychology: reward channels

**Transportation / absorption**
- van Laer, de Ruyter, Visconti, Wetzels (2014). "The Extended Transportation-Imagery Model: A Meta-Analysis of the Antecedents and Consequences of Consumers' Narrative Transportation." *Journal of Consumer Research*. [doi:10.1086/673383](https://doi.org/10.1086/673383). Meta-analysis of 132 effect sizes establishing transportation as a measurable construct.

**Reading flow**
- Thissen, Menninghaus, Schlotz (2018). "Measuring optimal reading experiences: The reading flow short scale." *Frontiers in Psychology*. [doi:10.3389/fpsyg.2018.02542](https://doi.org/10.3389/fpsyg.2018.02542). Establishes reading flow as distinct from absorption, with an inverted-U challenge-fit relationship to reading pleasure.


**Curiosity / uncertainty / prediction reward**
- Kidd & Hayden (2015). "The Psychology and Neuroscience of Curiosity." *Neuron*. [doi:10.1016/j.neuron.2015.09.010](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.neuron.2015.09.010). Curiosity as information-seeking and reward.
- Loewenstein (1994). "The Psychology of Curiosity: A Review and Reinterpretation." *Psychological Bulletin*. [doi:10.1037/0033-2909.116.1.75](https://doi.org/10.1037/0033-2909.116.1.75). Information-gap account of curiosity: uncertainty and missing knowledge drive attention.
- Litman (2005). "Curiosity and the Pleasures of Learning: Wanting and Liking New Information." *Cognition and Emotion*. [doi:10.1080/02699930541000101](https://doi.org/10.1080/02699930541000101). Distinguishes wanting information from enjoying obtained information.

**Social cognition and fiction**
- Dodell-Feder, Tamir (2018). "Fiction reading has a small positive impact on social cognition: A meta-analysis." *Journal of Experimental Psychology: General*. [doi:10.1037/xge0000395](https://doi.org/10.1037/xge0000395). Small positive effect; social simulation is a real engagement mechanism.
- Panero et al. (2016). "Does reading a single passage of literary fiction really improve theory of mind? An attempt at replication." *Journal of Personality and Social Psychology*. [pubmed 27642659](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/27642659/). Failed preregistered replication of the Kidd & Castano effect.
- Kidd & Castano (2018/2019) replication studies. [doi:10.1177/1948550618775410](https://doi.org/10.1177/1948550618775410). The original authors' own replication attempts.

**Aesthetic response and literariness**
- Wimmer, Lüdtke, Jacobs (2023). Studies on narrativity, literariness, and aesthetic response.
- van Peer, Hakemulder, Zyngier (2007). Work on foregrounding and stylistic attention.
- Hartung, Withers, Hagoort, Willems (2021). Style and suspense rely on different processing routes.

**Fiction vs factual framing**
- Hartung, Withers, Hagoort, Willems (2017). Same short story labeled fictional vs factual: no meaningful difference in immersion, appreciation, or reading time. [pubmed 28983269](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/28983269/). Fiction works because the reader adopts a simulation stance, not because the text disclaims factuality.

## NLP: documented AI writing failure modes

- Shaib, Barrow, Sun, Nenkova, Xu, Stern, Wallace (2024). "Detection and Measurement of Syntactic Templates in Generated Text." EMNLP 2024. [aclanthology.org/2024.emnlp-main.368](https://aclanthology.org/2024.emnlp-main.368/). Syntactic templates are copied from pretraining data and are not overwritten by fine-tuning or alignment.
- "The Price of Format: Diversity Collapse in LLMs." Findings of EMNLP 2025. [aclanthology.org/2025.findings-emnlp.836](https://aclanthology.org/2025.findings-emnlp.836/). Chat templates themselves suppress topical and semantic diversity on creative tasks, independent of content; prompt-side "be creative" instructions do not close the gap.
- "Creativity Has Left the Chat: The Price of Debiasing Language Models." [arxiv 2406.05587](https://arxiv.org/abs/2406.05587). RLHF-aligned models show lower token entropy and attractor-state behavior.
- "LLMs Exhibit Significantly Lower Uncertainty in Creative Writing Than Professional Writers." [arxiv 2602.16162](https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.16162). Instruction-tuned and reasoning models have lower token-level uncertainty than base models and than human professional writers, with a larger gap in creative writing than in functional domains.
- "Creating Suspenseful Stories: Iterative Planning with Large Language Models." EACL 2024. [aclanthology.org/2024.eacl-long.147](https://aclanthology.org/2024.eacl-long.147/). LLMs are unreliable for suspenseful story generation; iterative planning improves it.
- "Lost in Stories: Consistency Bugs in Long Story Generation by LLMs." [arxiv 2603.05890](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.05890). Factual and temporal consistency errors cluster in the middle of long narratives rather than at openings or endings.
- "Preference-tuned LLMs have greater effective semantic diversity despite lower lexical variety." [arxiv 2504.12522](https://arxiv.org/abs/2504.12522). Diversity and quality are separable; alignment changes the tradeoff surface rather than uniformly reducing creative capacity.

**Multi-agent creative architectures**
- "HoLLMwood: Unleashing the Creativity of Large Language Models in Screenwriting via Role Playing." Findings of EMNLP 2024. [aclanthology.org/2024.findings-emnlp.474](https://aclanthology.org/2024.findings-emnlp.474/). Role decomposition into writer / editor / actor roles improves screenwriting over strong single-agent baselines.

**Persona prompting interference**
- PRISM persona study. [arxiv 2603.18507](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.18507). Persona prompting activates instruction-following mechanisms that interfere with knowledge retrieval and reasoning on discriminative tasks: every persona variant reduced accuracy. Basis for the "no role identity" principle in agent and skill design.

## Craft tradition

- Hemingway, *Death in the Afternoon* (1932), chapter 16. Iceberg theory: "the writer may omit things that he knows and the reader, if the writer is writing truly enough, will have a feeling of those things as strongly as though the writer had stated them."
- Forster, *Aspects of the Novel* (1927). The reader as active participant; "what happens next and why" as organized causal progression.
- Gardner, *The Art of Fiction* (1983). "Vivid and continuous dream" as the governing metaphor for narrative transportation.
- Charles Baxter, *The Art of Subtext* (2007). The unsaid as a primary craft concern.
- James Wood, *How Fiction Works* (2008). Partial perspective, focalization, and free indirect style.
- Ursula K. Le Guin, "The Carrier Bag Theory of Fiction" (1986). Fiction as container of relations rather than conflict engine; truthful selection over minimalism.
- George Saunders, *A Swim in a Pond in the Rain* (2021). "Mutual assumption of intelligence" between writer and reader.
