# Character Development

Guidance for capturing exploration of character motivations, arcs, and relationships. Record what the author explores: don't write psychology they didn't state.

## Minimal Capture for Characters

**Motivation**: "Motivated by guilt" → record guilt. Don't elaborate on psychological mechanisms, attachment styles, or defense patterns. Keep at the level of depth the author explored.

**Backstory**: "Maybe Y happened in their past" → noted as possibility. Multiple backstory versions coexist. Most backstory stays undecided during story-planning.

**Character arc**: "Goes from ruthless to caring" → record both endpoints. Don't fill in the transformation beats the author didn't mention.

**Relationships**: "X and Y have tension" → note the dynamic. Don't invent incidents causing the tension or a resolution arc.

**Voice and personality**: "Sarcastic, uses humor to deflect" → capture those traits. Don't construct a psychological profile explaining why they're sarcastic.

## The Psychology Trap

The most common over-elaboration in character story-planning is inventing psychological depth the author didn't state. Author says "motivated by guilt" and the capture becomes a clinical profile with attachment theory, defense mechanisms, and therapeutic arcs.

The author might develop that depth later. Or they might keep the motivation simple. Either way, the story-planning note records what they said, not what a therapist would infer.

## Common Exploration Areas

### Motivation
Author figuring out: why does this character do what they do? Capture their answers and explorations. Don't add layers they didn't state.

### Character Arc
Author considering: where does this character start and end? Record endpoints, transformation moments if mentioned, and uncertainty about the path between them.

### Relationships
Author exploring: how do these characters interact? Note the dynamics as described. Multiple relationship trajectories can coexist.

### Backstory
Author story-planning: what happened before the story? Record possibilities. Multiple versions coexist until the author chooses.

### Voice
Author thinking through: how does this character talk and think? Record speech patterns, emotional expression, quirks as the author describes them.

## Multiple Character Versions

During story-planning, a character might exist in several contradictory states:

```markdown
# Character Options

Version A: Military background, stoic, tactically brilliant
Version B: Academic background, nervous, intellectually brilliant
Version C: Criminal background, charismatic, street-smart

Not decided yet: exploring which fits the story
```

All coexist. Don't advocate for one.
