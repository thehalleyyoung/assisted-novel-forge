# Revision Checklist

Systematic approach to polishing prose at every level.

## Structural Pass (Big Picture)

### Story Questions

- [ ] Is the opening hook compelling?
- [ ] Is the inciting incident clear and timely?
- [ ] Does the middle build tension or sag?
- [ ] Is the climax earned and satisfying?
- [ ] Does the ending resolve the central question?
- [ ] Are subplots integrated, not distracting?

### Pacing Check

- [ ] Any scenes that could be cut entirely?
- [ ] Any scenes that need expansion?
- [ ] Does tension rise and fall appropriately?
- [ ] Are quiet moments placed strategically?
- [ ] Is the ending rushed or dragged out?

### Scene Audit

For each scene, verify:
- [ ] Clear POV character with goal
- [ ] Obstacle to that goal
- [ ] Stakes if they fail
- [ ] Scene ends with change (status, knowledge, relationship)
- [ ] Necessary for plot or character development

## Character Pass

### Consistency

- [ ] Voice distinct and maintained throughout
- [ ] Actions align with established personality
- [ ] Reactions appropriate to character's knowledge
- [ ] Growth arc visible across story

### Motivation

- [ ] Want (conscious goal) is clear
- [ ] Need (unconscious) drives deeper conflict
- [ ] Choices make sense given motivation
- [ ] Obstacles are meaningful to this character

### Dialogue

- [ ] Each character sounds different
- [ ] Dialogue reveals character, not just information
- [ ] Subtext present in important conversations
- [ ] Characters don't explain themselves too much

## Scene-Level Pass

### Each Scene

- [ ] Grounded in time and place (setting details)
- [ ] Sensory details beyond just visual
- [ ] Conflict or tension present
- [ ] Begins late, ends early (trim entry/exit)
- [ ] Transitions smooth to next scene

### Show vs Tell Balance

- [ ] Key emotional moments are shown
- [ ] Transitions and summaries can tell
- [ ] Not over-showing mundane actions
- [ ] Interior thought serves purpose

## Line-Level Pass

### Sentence Craft

- [ ] Sentence length varies
- [ ] Strong verbs over weak verb + adverb
- [ ] Active voice predominates
- [ ] Parallel structure where appropriate
- [ ] No unintentional rhymes or repetition

### Word Choice

- [ ] Specific nouns over generic + adjectives
- [ ] Character-appropriate vocabulary
- [ ] No anachronisms (historical fiction)
- [ ] Jargon used correctly and sparingly

### Dialogue Mechanics

- [ ] "Said" used most often
- [ ] Action beats over dialogue tags where possible
- [ ] No adverbs on dialogue tags
- [ ] Each speaker clear without tagging every line

## Common Cuts

### Words to Eliminate

| Word | Problem | Solution |
|------|---------|----------|
| just | Weakens | Delete |
| really | Tells, doesn't show | Show instead |
| very | Lazy intensifier | Stronger word |
| suddenly | Telling surprise | Show character reaction |
| began to | Delays action | Just do the action |
| started to | Delays action | Just do the action |
| could see | Filter | Show what's seen |
| could hear | Filter | Show what's heard |
| felt | Telling emotion | Show body response |
| that | Often unnecessary | Read without it |

### Constructions to Revise

| Weak | Strong |
|------|--------|
| There was a dog in the yard | A dog paced the yard |
| She was running | She ran |
| He was tall and thin | He towered, thin as a reed |
| The room was dark | Darkness swallowed the room |
| She felt sad | Her chest ached |

## Final Polish

### Read Aloud

Listen for:
- [ ] Awkward phrasing
- [ ] Unnatural dialogue
- [ ] Rhythm problems
- [ ] Repeated words/sounds

### Technical

- [ ] Consistent tense
- [ ] Consistent POV
- [ ] Correct punctuation
- [ ] Proper formatting
- [ ] No typos

### Final Questions

- [ ] Does the title fit?
- [ ] Does the opening line hook?
- [ ] Does the ending resonate?
- [ ] Would I want to read this?

## Revision Order

1. **Structural** (don't polish sentences you might cut)
2. **Character** (ensure consistency before line work)
3. **Scene** (refine pacing and grounding)
4. **Line** (sentence and word level)
5. **Technical** (final proofread)

## Rest Between Passes

- After drafting: 1-7 days minimum
- After major revision: 2-3 days
- Before final proofread: Overnight
- Fresh eyes catch more
